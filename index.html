A STUDY ON IMPACTS OF DATA ANALYTICS IN AUTOMATING RISK MANAGEMENT

This study demonstrates the value of machine learning models in credit risk assessment. Random forest and gradient boosting models achieved superior accuracy (84% and 84.3%) compared to logistic regression and neural networks (83% and 83.3%). These models capture intricate variable relationships and handle missing data, improving predictions over traditional methods. While complex models like neural networks offer high predictive power, they pose interpretability challenges, which can affect regulatory compliance and customer trust. Despite this, machine learning significantly enhances credit risk practices, enabling better resource deployment, loss mitigation, and strategy optimization for financial institutions. Further research should address interpretability and explore advanced techniques like deep learning and natural language processing to improve model effectiveness. This study underscores the potential of machine learning in automating and enhancing credit risk assessment.
